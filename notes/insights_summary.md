# Insights Summary

## 1. Overall Churn

Observation:
The overall churn rate is approximately 26.5%.

Business meaning:
Around one quarter of customers in the dataset have churned, indicating a meaningful retention issue.

Possible action:
The company should monitor churn regularly and identify high-risk customer segments.

## 2. Contract Type

Observation:
Month-to-month customers have the highest churn rate.

Business meaning:
Customers without long-term contracts may have lower switching costs.

Possible action:
Offer contract upgrade incentives or loyalty discounts to month-to-month customers.

## 3. Tenure

Observation:
Customers with shorter tenure have higher churn.

Business meaning:
New customers may not yet have strong product attachment or service satisfaction.

Possible action:
Improve onboarding and early customer engagement.

## 4. Payment Method

Observation:
Customers using electronic check show higher churn.

Business meaning:
Payment method may reflect customer behavior, convenience issues, or service experience differences.

Possible action:
Review payment experience and provide smoother billing options.

## 5. Revenue Loss

Observation:
Some high-churn groups also contribute significant estimated monthly revenue loss.

Business meaning:
Retention should prioritize both churn probability and revenue impact.

Possible action:
Target high-value at-risk customers with personalized retention campaigns.
